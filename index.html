Women in the North

Since 2022 Ongoing

A multi-year collaborative thread at Kin Museum of Contemporary Art that foregrounds the conditions, knowledge, and dreams of women in the North. Olga’s contributions move between archival research, encounters, essays, exhibitions, and zines, including The Warmth of the Invisible Sun, devoted to Evgenia Patsia and the Kola North.

02

Archive · oral history · research

Archive of Artistic Life in Zapolyarye

Since 2023 Ongoing

An independent archive of contemporary art in Murmansk Oblast, launched by Olga within the Russian Art Archive Network. Beginning with the art scene in Apatity, it brings together testimonies, artworks, documents, and research spanning the mid-1970s through the 2000s.

About03 / 05

An independent curator, researcher, and educator based in Lyon.

Olga Shirokostup’s practice connects artistic research, archival work, and exhibition-making, with a focus on memory, knowledge production, and cultural narratives in the European High North.

Born in Severomorsk on the Kola Peninsula, Olga studied history at the Moscow School of Social and Economic Sciences and graduated from the Chto Delat’ School of Engaged Art. Her work moves between long-term institutional collaborations and independent research.

She is currently a curator within the European research project ARCA (Biocultural Heritage in Arctic Cities as a Potential Resource for Climate Adaptation, 2024–2027). She was co-curator of Arctic Art Forum V at the Natural History Museum in Oslo in 2026 and has been part of the Women in the North project, curated by Maria Lind, since 2022.

Between 2021 and 2023, Olga served as Chief Curator of the Radiance Centre for Contemporary Art in Apatity. Since 2023, she has curated the Archive of Artistic Life in Zapolyarye within RAAN. From 2020 to 2024, she was a Research Course Leader at Garage Academy. She is a recipient of a Robert Bosch Stiftung grant and a former participant in the Frame Curatorial Exchange Programme.
